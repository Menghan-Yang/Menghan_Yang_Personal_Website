Research Projects

Denoising in Naturalistic fMRI

This project investigates how different fMRI denoising strategies affect neural responses to complex naturalistic stimuli, such as movies or spoken stories. Our aim is to identify best practices that retain meaningful signal while minimizing motion- and physiology-related noise.

[GitHub] [Paper] [Poster]

Neural Stability Across Repeated Experiences

This study explores intra-individual variability and consistency in neural responses during repeated viewing of the same naturalistic stimulus. It examines how memory and attention shape neural dynamics across repeated exposure.

[GitHub] [Paper] [Poster]

Social Neuroscience Through Hyperscanning

This hyperscanning project investigates the neural mechanisms underlying co-viewing and conversations. Using fNIRS and fMRI, we study real-time neural synchrony and shared understanding during interactive social experiences.
