Status: PhD Candidate @ Dartmouth

Focus: Social Neuroscience

About Me.

I am a fourth-year Ph.D. student at Dartmouth College, working in the COSAN Lab with Professor Luke Chang. My research focuses on how people process and respond to naturalistic experiences, using multimodal data including fMRI, fNIRS, behavioral measures, physiological signals, and computational models.

Education.

I received my M.S. from the State Key Laboratory of Cognitive Neuroscience and Learning at Beijing Normal University, where I was advised by Professor Guosheng Ding. I earned my Bachelor's degree in Applied Psychology from Nanjing Normal University.

Interests.

I’m broadly interested in social interaction, human-machine interaction, and affective computing. I enjoy working with complex datasets—visualizing patterns, interpreting results, and generating insights that connect human behavior to real-world applications.

Personal.

I was born and raised in Jiangsu Province, China. After moving to the U.S., I’ve split my time between Hanover, New Hampshire, and the greater Seattle area. Outside of research, I enjoy playing tennis, photography, hiking, and traveling to explore new places.
